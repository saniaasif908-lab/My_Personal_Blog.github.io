# 🎓 My University Journey

This post documents my growth and experiences as a Computer Engineering student at UET.

---

## 1. First Day at University

### First Impressions
My first day at university was filled with excitement and confusion. I was very happy to start my Computer Engineering journey, but everything felt new, especially seeing that there were more boys than girls. I even went to the wrong department initially, but a senior guided me to the correct place, where I later found out that Computer Engineering students were temporarily placed in the Electrical Department. During the orientation session, I met my teachers, including my programming instructor, and for the first time I heard about coding, which made me both curious and nervous.

### Adjusting to Daily Life
As a day scholar, adjusting to daily travel and routine was challenging, but it marked the beginning of my journey into programming and university life.

---

## 2. First Experiences with Programming

### Entering the Lab
My first programming lab was exciting and intimidating. I felt shy and uncomfortable when I was asked to sit with a classmate who guided me through the basics.

### Learning Simple Commands
In the lab, I learned simple computer commands using CMD, such as creating and deleting folders, which helped me understand how computers work. This experience reduced my fear and built my initial confidence in programming.

---

## 3. Beginning Python

### Learning the Basics
Programming classes introduced me to Python. Initially, I could only understand basic concepts like input, output, and variables. Other topics were challenging and confusing.

### Quizzes and Progress
My quiz performance reflected my journey: good in the first quiz, poor in the second, and much improved in the third after extra effort. This taught me that programming requires consistency and patience.

---

## 4. Lab Challenges and Growth

### Adjusting Tools
Before mid exams, programming labs were challenging. I initially used lab computers but later brought my own laptop for comfort.

### Bridging Theory and Practice
The lectures moved faster than lab tasks, making learning confusing at times. However, completing tasks with practice and external resources strengthened my programming skills.

---

## 5. Discipline and Projects

### Learning Discipline
After mid exams, an incident in class taught me an important lesson about discipline: using my phone during a lecture led to a scolding, which motivated me to focus more.

### Machine Learning Project
We were assigned a group project on heart attack disease prediction. Working on this programming project introduced teamwork and practical application of theoretical knowledge.

---

## 6. Project Presentation

### Exciting and Stressful
Preparing and presenting our project in an exhibition was both exciting and stressful. Teachers asked many questions that tested our understanding.

### Overcoming Challenges
Presenting alone during the exhibition was nerve-wracking, but it improved my confidence and highlighted the importance of communication skills.

---

## 7. Independent Lab Work

### Open-ended Lab
Towards the end of the semester, the programming lab shuffled seats and required everyone to work independently.

### Facing Difficulties
Challenges like system issues and a power outage made work hard. Despite this, completing the task gave me confidence in my programming abilities.

---

## 8. University Life Beyond Coding

### Events and Adjustments
University life also involved managing different experiences. A welcome party clashed with a programming class, creating confusion. Traveling daily as a day scholar was tiring.

### Life Lessons
These moments, alongside academic responsibilities, were important in shaping my overall university experience.

---

## 9. First Semester Exams

### Stress and Preparation
Final exams, especially for programming, were challenging. I worked hard despite feeling stressed and overwhelmed.

### Achievement
My efforts paid off, performing very well and even topping the class in programming. This reinforced that consistency and hard work lead to success.

---

## 10. Second Semester and Iran War Challenges

### Database Learning
In the second semester, I was taught by the same instructor, this time for the database course. My understanding improved from the beginning. I learned practical skills such as SQL, WAMP server setup, and front-end/back-end integration, including proper documentation.

### Iran War and University Closure
Unfortunately, during this period, tensions in the region escalated due to the Iran war, leading to temporary university closures. Despite these disruptions, our second semester eventually started, and I continued learning programming and databases.

### Growth and Reflection
Although still building confidence, my journey from knowing nothing about coding to mastering programming and databases shows clear growth and development.

---

✨ *This journey reflects my growth as a student, coder, leader, and IEEE member, combining technical expertise, academic achievements, personal development, and resilience in the face of regional challenges.*
